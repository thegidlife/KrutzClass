#SAFETY REGULATIONS

What even are Spy Drones?

Up to this point, we have not actually explained what a spy drone is. The name practically explains itself. It is a drone (unmanned vehicle), used for the purpose of detecting the location and activity of the enemy.

Now, the term is mostly used in the military, for those breathtakingly expensive, super high-tech drones we hear about in the news when they take out an entire terrorist black market in the middle of the desert.

Are they Safe?

YES! Well as long as...

1) Legislators should follow a property rights approach to aerial surveillance.

2) Legislators should craft simple, duration-based surveillance legislation that will limit the aggregate amount of time the government may surveil a specific individual.

3) Legislators should adopt data retention procedures that require heightened levels of suspicion and increased procedural protections for accessing stored data gathered by aerial surveillance.

4) Legislators should enact transparency and accountability measures

5) Legislators should recognize that technology such as geofencing and auto-redaction, may make aerial surveillance by drones more protective of privacy than human surveillance
